Liangpi: A Noodle Delicacy from Northwest China

Liangpi is a traditional snack common in Qinghai and Northwest China. The preparation involves washing wheat flour dough in water until the starch and gluten are separated. After sedimentation, the starch water is steamed into liangpi, and the gluten can be used for cold dishes or stir-fries. It is smooth and elastic in texture, and served with various seasonings such as garlic paste, chili oil, and sesame paste. Liangpi is a satisfying dish for both main courses and snacks.

Qinghai Yogurt: A Perfect Balance of Sweet and Sour

Qinghai yogurt is a distinctive dairy product from Qinghai. It is known for its thick texture, perfect balance of sweet and sour taste, and unique production process. On the streets of Qinghai, people can often be seen holding bowls of yogurt, enjoying this delicious treat from the plateau. The process of making Qinghai yogurt is precise, using high-quality fresh milk and fermented with lactic acid bacteria. The resulting yogurt is rich and creamy, offering a smooth taste that blends the freshness of milk with the benefits of probiotics.

Hand-Pulled Lamb: A Representative of Qinghai's Meat Cuisine

Hand-pulled lamb is a distinctive meat dish from Qinghai. It uses high-quality lamb meat that is carefully prepared to ensure tenderness and flavor without being greasy. Typically, people grab the lamb with their hands, dip it into special seasonings, and take big bites to savor the deliciousness. The process is simple but requires careful attention to cooking time and seasoning balance. Fresh lamb is boiled, then fried in hot oil until golden brown.

Offal Soup: A Breakfast Favorite in Qinghai

Offal soup is a traditional breakfast food in Qinghai. It uses offal such as heart, liver, lungs, and intestines from cattle and sheep, which are cooked in a rich and flavorful broth. In the mornings in Qinghai, people love to enjoy this warm soup with flatbreads or fried dough sticks, feeling the warmth and satisfaction from the highlands.

Huahua: A Delicious and Beautiful Hui Cuisine

Huahua is a traditional Hui food from Qinghai. It is made from high-quality flour, water, and yeast. After fermentation, it is kneaded into a smooth dough, rolled into thin sheets, cut into small pieces, and deep-fried until golden and crispy. The finished huahua is visually appealing and offers a variety of flavors, making it a perfect choice for festivals and everyday meals.

Qingke Tsampa: A Traditional Tibetan Food

Qingke tsampa is a traditional food of the Tibetan people in Qinghai. It is made by mixing high-quality qingke flour with water and butter to form a smooth dough, which is then shaped into strips or small pieces for consumption. Tsampa has a fragrant, chewy texture and is a nutritious food commonly found on Tibetan dining tables.

Yak Jerky: A Unique Travel Snack from Qinghai

Yak jerky is a popular travel snack from Qinghai, made from yak meat. It is carefully prepared and dried to produce a chewy, flavorful snack. It is an excellent choice for replenishing energy during travel or satisfying one's hunger on the go.

Here lies Qinghai Lake, the largest saltwater lake in China, with vast grasslands and majestic Qilian Mountains, forming an unparalleled natural landscape. Qinghai is not only one of the birthplaces of Tibetan culture, but also a paradise for nature enthusiasts and explorers.
